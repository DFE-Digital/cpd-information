What is an NPQ?

NPQs have been designed to support the continous professional development of teachers and leaders throughout their careers.

An NPQ is similar to a level 7 qualification such as a postgraduate master's degree. Study typically involves completing a series of modules and passing a written assessment at the end.

How long is an NPQ?

NPQ courses take between 12 months and 21 months to complete depending on which one you're doing.

Are there different types of NPQ?

We want all teachers to have options for high-quality, evidence-informed, practical professional development. To reflect this, there are 2 types of NPQ:

  • specialist courses for anyone who wants to focus on different aspects of classroom practice and school life
  • leadership courses aimed at developing your skills and knowledge as you progress into more senior roles

What an NPQ involves

NPQs are typically split into separate modules, with a mix of self-study and group coaching sessions, as well as face-to-face sessions alongside other participants.

You'll have access to online content providing flexible learning that can be done at a time to suit your pace.

On average you will need to spend between 1 and 2 hours per week working towards completing a course. Where possible, you should do this during dedicated career development time in school hours.

Why take an NPQ

Completing an NPQ could help you:

  • deepen your expertise in your chosen area
  • improve pupil outcomes by implementing what you've learnt
  • gain knowledge that can be shared with colleagues
