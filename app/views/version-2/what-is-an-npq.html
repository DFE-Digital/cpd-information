National professional qualifications (NPQs)

What is a national professional qualification (NPQ)?

NPQs have been designed to support the professional development of teachers and leaders beyond the first few years of teaching.

We want to help all teachers and school leaders to continuously develop their expertise throughout their careers so every child gets the best start in life.

An NPQ is similar to a level 7 qualification such as a postgraduate master's degree. Study typically involves completing a series of modules and passing a written assessment at the end.

How long is an NPQ?

NPQs courses take between 12 months and 18 months to complete depending on which one you're doing.

Are there different types of NPQ?

We want all teachers to have options for high-quality, evidence-informed, practical professional development. To reflect this, there are 2 types of NPQ:

  • specialist courses for anyone who wants to focus on different aspects of classroom practice and school life
  • leadership courses aimed at developing leadership skills and knowledge

What an NPQ involves

NPQs are typically split into separate modules, with a mix of self-study and group coaching sessions, as well as face-to-face sessions alongside other participants.

You'll have access to online content providing flexible learning that can be done at a time to suit your pace. On average you will need to spend between 1 and 2 hours per week working towards completing a course. Where possible, you should do this during dedicated career development time in school hours.
